When you fall in love, your brain releases dopamine and oxytocin — chemicals that make you feel joy and comfort.

Butterflies in the stomach are real! They happen because of adrenaline released when you’re near someone special.

Couples who laugh together often have stronger bonds. Even though we haven’t met yet, I know your laughter will be my favorite sound.

Romantic love activates the brain like an addiction — dopamine floods the reward pathways, similar to how the brain responds to certain drugs.

Holding hands with someone you love can reduce physical pain and stress by syncing your heartbeats and brain waves.

Couples who look into each other’s eyes for just 3 minutes can feel significantly closer and more connected.

Bananas are berries, but strawberries are not. Botanically speaking, bananas qualify as berries while strawberries do not.

Sharks existed before trees. Sharks have been around for more than 400 million years, while trees appeared about 350 million years ago.

The Eiffel Tower can grow taller in summer. Heat expands the iron, making the tower up to 15 cm higher during hot days.

Octopuses have three hearts, and two stop beating when they swim.

Sloths can hold their breath longer than dolphins — up to 40 minutes compared to a dolphin’s 10 minutes.

Honey never spoils. Archaeologists have found pots of honey in ancient Egyptian tombs that are over 3,000 years old and still edible.
